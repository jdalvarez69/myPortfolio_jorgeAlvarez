Direction

Why project management

Project management sits at the center of how work gets done — defining scope, coordinating people, tracking progress, and closing the gap between intent and delivery. As a Business and Information Systems student, I'm building the foundation for exactly that.

Role Research

What project managers do

Goals & Scope

Define what the project is trying to accomplish and set clear boundaries on what is and isn't included.

Timelines & Tasks

Break work into concrete steps, assign ownership, and build a schedule around dependencies and realistic estimates.

Stakeholder Communication

Keep the right people informed. Communicate status, decisions, and changes consistently.

Risks & Blockers

Identify what could go wrong early. Monitor blockers and resolve or escalate to keep work moving.

Resources & Responsibilities

Coordinate people, tools, and budget. Keep ownership and resource needs clear.

Documentation & Decisions

Record what was decided, why, and by whom. Documentation keeps teams aligned.
