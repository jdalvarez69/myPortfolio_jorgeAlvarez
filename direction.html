Direction

Why project management

I'm interested in project management because the role sits at the center of how work actually gets done. Project managers define what needs to happen, coordinate who does it, track where things break down, and make sure the result matches the original intent. That combination of planning, communication, and execution appeals to me.

I'm a Business and Information Systems student, so I'm building foundational knowledge in both business operations and technical systems. Project management connects those two areas in a way that matches where I want to go.

Role Research

What project managers do

Goals & Scope

Define what the project is trying to accomplish and set clear boundaries around what is included and what is not.

Timelines & Tasks

Break work into concrete steps, assign ownership, and build a schedule that accounts for dependencies and realistic estimates.

Stakeholder Communication

Keep the right people informed at the right times. Communicate status, decisions, and changes clearly and consistently.

Risks & Blockers

Identify what could go wrong before it does. Monitor for blockers and resolve or escalate them so work keeps moving.

Resources & Responsibilities

Coordinate people, tools, and budget. Make sure each team member knows what they own and has what they need.

Documentation & Decisions

Record what was decided, why, and by whom. Good documentation keeps teams aligned and creates a record for future reference.
